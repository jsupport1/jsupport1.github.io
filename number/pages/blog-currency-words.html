Writing Currency in Words: USD, GBP, EUR, and INR

When dealing with international invoices or contracts, getting the currency format right in words is a legal necessity. Different currencies have different fractional units, which must be accurately reflected.

Major Currencies and Their Fractions

  • US Dollar (USD): The main unit is Dollars, and the fractional unit is Cents. E.g., $150.50 is written as "One Hundred Fifty Dollars and Fifty Cents."
  • British Pound (GBP): The main unit is Pounds, and the fractional unit is Pence. E.g., £75.25 is "Seventy-Five Pounds and Twenty-Five Pence."
  • Euro (EUR): The main unit is Euros, and the fractional unit is Cents. E.g., €200.10 is "Two Hundred Euros and Ten Cents."
  • Indian Rupee (INR): The main unit is Rupees, and the fractional unit is Paise. E.g., ₹500.75 is "Five Hundred Rupees and Seventy-Five Paise."
