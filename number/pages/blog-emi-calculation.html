How EMI Calculation Works: The Math Behind Loans

When you take out a loan for a home, car, or personal expenses, you are required to pay it back in Equated Monthly Installments (EMI). But how exactly do banks calculate this figure?

The EMI Formula

The mathematical formula used to calculate EMI is:

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]

Where:

  • P: Principal loan amount
  • R: Rate of interest per month (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)
  • N: Loan tenure in months

Principal vs Interest

An EMI consists of two parts: the principal repayment and the interest payment. In the early years of your loan, a large portion of the EMI goes towards paying the interest. As the years progress, the principal component of the EMI increases.

Why Use an EMI Calculator?

Manually calculating EMI is tedious and prone to errors. Using an EMI Calculator allows you to instantly see your monthly payment, total interest payable, and the amortization schedule, helping you make informed financial decisions.
